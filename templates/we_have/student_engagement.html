ZAKOVAT Club Activities at SamSIFL

At the Samarkand State Institute of Foreign Languages (SamSIFL), ZAKOVAT intellectual quiz competitions are regularly organized through the initiative of the Youth Union and various academic departments. These events aim to enhance students’ critical thinking, teamwork, and general knowledge.

The institute also has an active ZAKOVAT Club, which operates continuously throughout the academic year. The club not only prepares teams for internal tournaments but also represents SamSIFL in city and regional intellectual competitions. Through these activities, students develop their intellectual abilities, communication skills, and creative problem-solving capacity.

ZAKOVAT at SamSIFL has become an essential part of the university’s intellectual and cultural life, fostering a spirit of curiosity, friendship, and healthy competition among students.

Booklovers' Club Activities at SamSIFL

REPORT ON THE IMPLEMENTATION OF THE FOURTH INITIATIVE AT SAMSIFL

The fourth initiative of the Five Important Initiatives put forward by the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan is aimed at enhancing the spiritual and cultural awareness of young people and promoting reading culture among them.

In line with this initiative, the Samarkand State Institute of Foreign Languages (SamSIFL) regularly organizes various activities to foster students’ interest in literature and to ensure their free time is spent meaningfully.

One of the key projects within this framework is the “Discussion of a Literary Work” event, which is held every week. During these discussion sessions, students analyze and exchange opinions about famous literary works, share their reflections, and explore the moral and cultural values reflected in the texts

These activities play an important role in developing students’ critical thinking, communication skills, and love for reading. They also contribute to the creation of a spiritually rich academic environment at SamSIFL, encouraging young people to value knowledge, creativity, and culture.

Overall, the implementation of the fourth initiative at SamSIFL has proven to be an effective way to nurture intellectual curiosity, strengthen cultural awareness, and support the personal growth of students.

Student Theater Studio Club Activities at SamSIFL

REPORT ON THE ESTABLISHMENT OF THE STUDENT THEATER STUDIO AT SAMSIFL

In order to further enrich the cultural and creative life of students, a Student Theater Studio has been established at the Samarkand State Institute of Foreign Languages (SamSIFL). The initiative aims to discover and support talented young people, enhance their artistic skills, and promote the values of national culture and creativity among students.

More than 50 students from various faculties have actively joined the Theater Studio. They regularly participate in acting workshops, rehearsals, and stage performances organized under the guidance of experienced mentors and teachers

The activities of the Student Theater Studio provide students with an opportunity to express themselves artistically, develop their self-confidence, and strengthen their teamwork and communication abilities. Performances prepared by the studio are often showcased during institute-wide cultural events and festive celebrations, receiving great interest and appreciation from the audience.

The establishment of the Theater Studio at SamSIFL reflects the institute’s commitment to supporting youth creativity and fostering a vibrant cultural environment on campus. It serves as an important platform for nurturing talented, expressive, and culturally aware young individuals.

Booklover girls

In line with the fourth direction of the Five Important Initiatives proposed by the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan — which aims to enhance youth spirituality and promote reading culture among students — the “Booklover Student” (“Kitobxon Talaba”) Contest was held at the Samarkand State Institute of Foreign Languages (SamSIFL).

At the beginning of the event, members of the organizing committee explained the rules and conditions of the contest to all participants. Then, in accordance with the competition regulations, students’ knowledge and reading comprehension were tested.

Among the talented participants, Marjona Tolibova and O‘g‘iloy Boltaboyeva, first-year students of the Faculty of English Philology and Translation Studies, distinguished themselves with their knowledge and insight, earning 50 points each and becoming the winners of the contest.

The “Booklover Student” competition serves as an important initiative aimed at guiding young people toward knowledge and intellectual growth, encouraging them to make books their closest companions. Such activities play a vital role in developing reading culture, improving the quality of education, and fostering a spiritually rich learning environment at SamSIFL.
